Exciting news! Unhackathon is expanding!

Instead of a single fall hackathon, Unhackathon will run two events this year: A 12-hour event in the fall on September 5th, and an overnight hackathon early in the spring semester from January 31-February 1st. Unhackathon’s two goals have always been to increase the inclusiveness of our own events, and to help other hackathons do the same in innovative ways. After reading hundreds of applications, we’ve decided that this event schedule will accomplish our aims the best. At our Fall event next weekend, we’ll be able to welcome more new hackers - including, for the first time, high school students! Expert hackers and beginners alike will get to try our Springboard Projects, a team of great mentors from the NYC tech community will encourage and advise each step of the way, and everyone will start the hackathon season on a positive note. We’ll spend the rest of the fall semester helping other organizing teams, and then be back with the new year for an even bigger overnight event!

All our traditional activities - shirt painting, tech talks, Ladies Storm Hackathons meetup, and of course build-your-own Chipotle burrito, will feature at both events. You’ll also get a chance to meet the teams of our wonderful local sponsors.

If you were accepted to Unhackathon already, and can’t attend our one-day event, your application will be considered specially for the spring overnight hackathon - no need to apply twice. Expect an email from us this week with more details. Apply Now!

What is Unhackathon?

When we first created Unhackathon, we wanted to make hackathons even more inclusive and fun! Our first order of business was making sure that everyone felt safe, had good food to eat, and time to sleep. We then moved onto improving awards, ensuring that they were based on technical ambition, thoughtful code, inspired design, and potential for improvements beyond the hackathon. This year, we want to go further in making the event the funnest possible for everyone involved. We want everyone, from enthusiastic beginners to grizzled coding veterans to come home with something they're proud of, and something new they've learned.

We hope to encourage attendees to learn new skills and technologies by making API resources, hardware, and interesting data sets available to everyone. We’ll also have everything you’ll need to be productive - many inspiring work spaces to fit your mood, energy drinks and healthy snacks and meals, and advice from the greatest mentors ever. Our mentors come from the best NYC tech companies and startups, and will be around the whole time to help you with everything from ideas to bugs.

We want our hackers to learn a ton in a short time within a welcoming environment. All Unhackathon participants are expected to abide by our Code of Conduct. We place great value on diversity, of ideas and of backgrounds. Are you eager to learn something new? We would love for you to join us.

Should I attend Unhackathon?

Absolutely! We want to see college students from every background, whether or not you have any programming skill. We will be judging applications primarily on thoughtfullness and enthusiasm, and not based on whether you've already written a C compiler in your spare time. But if you have accomplished everything, we'd still love you to come and help spread the hackathon cheer!

Unhackathon is open to all college students. You should be interested in coding, as that is what you will be doing at Unhackathon. However, you do not need to be a skilled programmer; Unhackathon is intended to be a learning environment. You should not come to Unhackathon in search of others to do free work on your app idea, but if you'd like to give it a try coding it up yourself we'd be happy to help you out. You don't need a team to attend; we'll happily match you up with a team when you arrive and you are also welcome to work solo. We can also help you brainstorm project ideas if you don't already have one in mind.

On the fence? Here are some statistics and anecdotes from last year's event:

  • Half of of our attendees were attending their first hackathon.
  • We had more women attending than any other student hackathon we've attended. 20% of our attendees were women and we hope to improve upon this number this year.
  • A number of attendees told us this was the "best" and/or "most welcoming" hackathon they've attended. Or that they learned more in a day at Unhackathon than they had in a semester of school.
  • One hacker programmed a simulation of a neuron, another team created a game highlighting the hardships of being a single mother. Whatever your idea, we'd love to help your project become reality.

We hope we've convinced you! Come with ready minds and pour your heart into creating something great.

Our Goal: Go home feeling proud.

Hackathons are great for bringing people together. Thousands of students arrive at hackathons each year, ready to learn and build. But demos are often mostly spectator events. Somewhere between the opening ceremonies and the final crunch, hackers drop out because they run into a problem they can't fix, they can't come up with a workable project idea, or they just burn out. It's our goal at Unhackathon to have every hacker demo if they want, and go home with a project they're proud of - no matter how small it is. Our Springboard projects are a great way to start a project you can expand on even after the hackathon. If you're on a team building something completely new, we've got your back. We'll have plenty of expert mentors in the fields that you're most interested in, and a special team to help you debug when crunch time gets close.

Stuck, solo, or in it to learn? Try a Springboard Project.

Springboard projects are an Unhackathon alternative to the traditional hackathon project. If you're interested in learning a new skill or technology, choose from one of our projects designed to get you learning by building a real project. These aren't tutorials like you've seen them online. Instead, you'll work on a project for 3-4 hours that teaches you the basics of Arduino programming, natural language processing, or design, just to name a few, and then spring off in your own direction for the rest of the hacking. If you were stuck on a project idea before, you'll be full of ideas by the time you're ready to expand. You'll be supported by mentors who are experts on your project, and encouraged by those around you working from the same springboard. This is a perfect opportunity if you're new to coding or hackathons, or if you're interested in working alone or in a pair.
